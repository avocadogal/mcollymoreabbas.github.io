Where to Start?

Recycling used and un-used items is always a good way to protect the environment from the harmful effects of landfills and litter, but knowing what to recycle and where to recycle can be quite the task if you don't already know where to start. First, it's important to know what to recycle and where to recycle it. often times it will be advised against collection crertain recyclable materials at your home, in which case, you'll have to know where to bring that material so it can be recycled.

Below are some helpful pieces information regarding the appropriate recycling techniques as well as a table that should give you an idea about what can be picked up by the your local recycling service, what needs to be brought to your local center, and what materials aren't recyclable, but can be composted instead. Though many recycling it should be noted that what is consiidered "recyclable" varries from place to place, so make sure to check your the guidlines of your local service before embarking on your recycling adventures.

Remember:

  • Flatten recycled items for easy handeling and storage later on
  • Check your area's recycling guidlines
  • If you're not sure about a particular item, don't recycle it
  • Bring your recyclables to places that actually recycle them; check for downcycling
  • Remember to clean/ empty glass bottles before recycling
  • Some curbside recycling programs may require you to sort the glass by color
  • Broken and unwashed glass cannot be recycled at home; bring to a recycling center
  • Other thigs that can't be recycled at home:
    • Ceramics
    • Heat-resistant glass
    • Mirror/window glass
    • Crystal
